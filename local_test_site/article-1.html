Agentic AI refers to systems that do more than answer a single prompt. They can break a goal into steps, decide what information they need, use external tools, and revise their approach as new results arrive.

In product teams, this shows up in agents that triage support tickets, investigate incidents, draft code changes, and compile research summaries. The value comes from reducing the amount of manual coordination required for repetitive knowledge work.

Well-designed agentic workflows usually combine a model with clear instructions, bounded tools, memory for intermediate state, and checks that verify whether a step succeeded before moving on.

The biggest practical challenges are reliability and control. Agents can fail when they misunderstand the task, call the wrong tool, or continue past a sensible stopping point. Strong guardrails and human review are still important for high-impact actions.

As the ecosystem matures, teams are focusing less on fully autonomous agents and more on useful semi-autonomous systems that save time while keeping people in the loop.
